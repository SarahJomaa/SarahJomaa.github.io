Brendon Urie

Photoshop

This painting is a redraw of a frame from Brendon Urie's music video, "Death of a Bachelor." It was emotionally evocative, and the lighting and composition held some dramaticism. I'd been wanting to experiment with a rough, painterly look, and I knew that style would work well with this piece. It provided a great opportunity to practice a new technique.

Dragon Age: Cole Tarot

Photoshop

In the video game Dragon Age: Inquisition, each party member has a set of tarot cards to represent their current status in the storyline. Cole is one of my favorite characters and this tarot card was particularly compelling to me. In my interpretation, I used more vivid colors, higher contrast, and rougher brushes. I wanted to bring more emphasis to the chaotic nature of the character and his emotional arc, as opposed to the cleaner lines and lighter feeling in the original.

Colin Morgan

Photoshop

This piece is a portrait of the actor Colin Morgan. I wanted to challenge myself; my aim was to blur the distinction between foreground and background—I wanted the subject to seem as though he could take one step back and disappear. His dark hair was particularly important to this goal since it blends so well into the background. I had to carefully suggest the shape of it with highlights of individual sections and strands. It was difficult but very rewarding.

Existential deconstruction

Photoshop

Danny Phantom is a half ghost character from a cartoon from my childhood. The anatomy was interesting—as a ghost his body is made of ectoplasm. The show often danced around darker themes, but being a children’s cartoon, made light of them with jokes. I wanted to explore those themes more seriously, so I played with the idea of his ectoplasm breaking down and making his structure unstable. Suggesting he could melt, and his body parts could detach and dissolve if his core destabilized, leans into elements of body horror.
Animating the glow and the dripping ectoplasm was also a challenge because I was learning to use entirely new tools and features in Photoshop, but it was deeply satisfying to see the effect once they were finished.

Excalibur

Illustrator

The sword design is based on the sword used in the BBC show Merlin, and the butterfly is a callback to a pivotal scene in the series finale. Together they represent power and moderation of power, assertiveness and gentleness, justice and mercy. In the background, if you look closely, you can spot constellations. I included ones I believe represent themes from the show as well as the two main characters, King Arthur and Merlin. This piece was very, very time consuming because of the level of attention to detail that went into it; even the tiniest marks are each carefully, manually placed and adjusted.

Frog

Photoshop

The frog was one of my first attempts at hyperrealism—I spent most of the time on the frog zoomed in working with a 1 pixel brush. I'm proud of how well it holds up from a distance as well as zoomed in close. The plant proved to be more difficult than the frog itself, which was unexpected at the time. Now I understand that it required much more subtlety in its approach than the frog, which had more obvious and distinct focal points.
