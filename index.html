It's Nice To Meet You
I'm Sarah Jomaa, UX Designer, Researcher, and artist. Welcome to my site!

About

I graduated with a degree in User Experience from the School of Information at the University of Michigan - Ann Arbor, with a minor in Art & Design. For over a year now, I've been working as a UX Designer at Ally. One of my greatest strengths is being highly empathetic, and I bring that to the table in my UX work. I believe understanding others is at the core of UX.

Background

My hometown of Dearborn, Michigan, is a diverse, friendly, and thriving intercultural community. I have the unique perspective of being the daughter of immigrants, of growing up both Arab and American, and of speaking two languages: English with society, and Arabic with family. It's for these reasons and more that I'm passionate about diversity, equality, and progressive societal change.

Career

I've taken every new project in my career at Ally and my education at the University of Michigan as an opportunity to grow my knowledge and hone my skillset, because as people and technology evolve, so must we as designers. I aspire in my work to make that connection between people and technology seamless, intuitive, and constantly adaptive.

UX Portfolio

Most of the projects shown here come from my internship at the Office of Technology Transfer (OTT) where I worked for over a year. OTT provides UX design and consultation for startups, primarily for those in the healthcare industry, a field with often precise and specialized requirements. There I worked with my fellow intern to meet with clients, discuss their project ideas and parameters, and put together UX workbooks for them to use in grant applications and proposals. These workbooks typically consisted of use case definitions, two unique wireframe prototypes, and an interactive prototype or regulatory assessment as needed.

Anegene

Perioperative Healthcare Website
November 2018
Use Cases, Wireframing

Anegene is a platform that aims to create a comprehensive and intuitive resource to address the needs of perioperative healthcare providers. It enables these providers to identify patient attributes that may affect their care. Using these attributes, Anegene presents the risk of perioperative complications per individual for more accurate and personalized healthcare.
My coworker and I worked closely with our client, an anesthesiologist, to create use cases that reflect actual provider and patient needs. This project required a high attention to detail due to the precision required in perioperative careâ€“I went through multiple iterations and feedback sessions with the client to ensure my prototypes were sufficiently accurate and thorough.

Burson Project

Cardiovascular Intensive Care Unit (CVICU) Communication App
September 2019
Use Cases, Wireframing, Interactive Prototype

The ambition of the Burson Project is to come up with a solution for the inadequate communication system currently in place for CVICU patients. The majority of these patients experience difficulty or complete inability to communicate at some point during the course of their treatment. During these periods CVICU's typically use communication boards, which are ineffective and often frustrating for patients, their families, and their healthcare providers.

I had to be creative with my approach, as this app's users have accessibility needs that are very different from the average person. It was challenging, but rewardingâ€”I had to reevaluate many UX standards and come up with unique alternative systems to meet our users' unique needs.

Safesense

Helmet Impact Monitoring App | November 2018 | Use Cases, Wireframing

Safesense is an app created for athletic coaches and trainers to work in tandem with sensors in football playerâ€™s helmets. It feeds them live impact information so they can monitor their playersâ€™ health and minimize injuries. The app collects a large variety of data, so it required careful organization to make the information digestible. Safesense has a variety of users with different roles and responsibilities. To help address these needs, I developed personas to help guide the design for multiple roles such as modes for coaches, medical staff, and parents. I really enjoyed working on this project because of the growing prevalence of CTE and concussion awareness in sports media, and it was fascinating seeing what was being done and being able to contribute as well.

Dragon Age: Cole Tarot


Photoshop

In the video game Dragon Age: Inquisition, each party member has a set of tarot cards to represent their current status in the storyline. Cole is one of my favorite characters and this tarot card was particularly compelling to me. In my interpretation, I used more vivid colors, higher contrast, and rougher brushes. I wanted to bring more emphasis to the chaotic nature of the character and his emotional arc, as opposed to the cleaner lines and lighter feeling in the original.
